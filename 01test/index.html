Digital Citizenship

What is Digital Citizenship?

Digital citizenship is the responsible, safe, and respectful use of technology and the internet, involving ethical behavior, critical thinking, and understanding your rights and responsibilities in online communities

What is Digital Law?

Digital law (or cyber law) is the body of legal rules and principles governing activities, rights, and responsibilities in the digital world

What is AUP?

AUP most commonly stands for Acceptable Use Policy, a set of rules defining permitted and prohibited uses of an organization's network, devices, and data

What is Plagiarism?

Plagiarism is presenting someone else's words, ideas, or work as your own without giving proper credit

Essentially committing intellectual theft by failing to cite the original source

What is Academic Dishonesty?

Academic dishonesty is any act that gives a student an unfair advantage in learning or assessment, violating ethical standards and institutional policies

What are the rules for AI Generated Content?

Transparency is required when using AI-generated materials. Any published content generated or substantially influenced by AI should include an explicit disclosure, clearly visible to the audience
