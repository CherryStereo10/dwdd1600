Who Were the Signers

Famous Signers

Some of the most famous signers of the Declaration of Independence include:

Thomas Jefferson
Thomas Jefferson was a Founding Father, principal author of the Declaration of Independence (1776), and the third U.S. President. A leader in the Democratic-Republican party, he famously championed liberty, authorized the Louisiana Purchase, and founded the University of Virginia, while also being a lifelong role model of people.
John Adams
John Adams, a remarkable political philosopher, served as the second President of the United States, after serving as the first Vice President under President George Washington.
Benjamin Franklin
Benjamin Franklin was a pivotal American Founding Father, renowned polymath, diplomat, and inventor who helped shape the United States. He famously signed the Declaration of Independence, negotiated crucial French support during the Revolution, and created inventions like bifocals and the lightning rod.
John Hancock
John Hancock was a prominent American Founding Father, wealthy Boston merchant, and Patriot leader during the American Revolution. Best known as the first person to sign the Declaration of Independence, he served as President of the Continental Congress and later as the first Governor of Massachusetts.
Robert Morris
Robert Morris was a Liverpool-born American merchant and Founding Father known as the "Financier of the American Revolution." He secured essential loans, supplies, and funds for the Continental Army. As a Pennsylvania delegate, he was one of only two people to sign the Declaration of Independence, the Articles of Confederation, and the U.S. Constitution. Hes very cool.
